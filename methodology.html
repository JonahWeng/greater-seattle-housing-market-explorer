Data Methodology

Greater Seattle Housing Market Explorer · Last updated July 20, 2026

← Back to the interactive map

Coverage

  • 78 incorporated Census Places in the Tacoma–Everett–North Bend corridor across King, Snohomish, and Pierce counties.
  • All 78 cities match 2025 U.S. Census Place boundaries and Washington OFM population estimates.
  • Current Redfin city-level market data is available for 77 of 78 cities in this snapshot. Index does not have a current Redfin city row.

Housing market sources

  • Redfin Data Center: median sale price, price per square foot, active listings, inventory, months of supply, homes sold, days on market, and sale-to-list ratio.
  • Washington State Office of Financial Management: annual city population estimates.
  • U.S. Census Bureau 2025 Places: incorporated city boundaries.
  • U.S. Census ACS B25034: housing stock by year built.

Time periods

  • All-residential price and price-per-square-foot comparisons use April–June three-month seasonal windows.
  • Property-type comparisons use Redfin monthly data through May 2026 and compare the same seasonal months.
  • The 1-year, 3-year, and 5-year views compare consistent months rather than a single month.
  • 2026 sales are labeled YTD when used and are not mixed with full-year totals.

Property types

  • All Residential
  • Single Family Residential
  • Townhouse
  • Condo/Co-op

Sample and display rules

  • Lecture-safe: hides price-change values below the applicable sample threshold.
  • Show all with caution: shows available raw values and marks low-sample values with an asterisk.
  • All-residential seasonal price changes use a minimum of 50 sales.
  • City-property-type seasonal comparisons use a minimum of 10 sales.
  • Missing values remain blank and are never changed to zero.
  • Color scales may be percentile-capped for readability; labels and detail values remain exact.

Inventory and backlog

Months of Supply is the only metric used to describe market backlog. Active Listings describes market scale and is not used as a substitute for backlog.

Map interpretation

  • Gray city areas are below the selected sample rule, unavailable, or outside the selected city scope.
  • Open gaps are unincorporated county land. Census Place boundaries do not tile an entire county.
  • A large geographic area does not necessarily have a large population or many transactions.
